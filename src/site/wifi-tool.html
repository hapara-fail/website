ChromeOS WiFi Extractor

This tool has two methods for finding saved WiFi passwords. Choose the one that best fits your situation.

This tool is not optimized for mobile devices. For best results, please use a desktop or laptop computer.

Sync Internals Extractor

For networks you saved yourself (e.g. home WiFi).

Network Policy Extractor

For networks set up by an admin (e.g. school WiFi).

SIPE: Step 1 of 4

Open Sync Internals

In a new tab, navigate to chrome://sync-internals and click the "Search" tab at the top.

SIPE: Step 2 of 4

Enter SSID (Optional)

To make finding your network easier, type its name (SSID) below. This is optional, but highly recommended.

SIPE: Step 3 of 4

Find & Copy Data

In the sync-internals search box, type wifi_ and press Enter. Find your network in the list on the left (if you entered an SSID, look for the term below) and click it. Copy the full JSON data that appears on the right.

SIPE: Step 4 of 4

Extract Credentials

Paste the copied JSON data into the box below and click Extract to show the credentials.

NPPE: Step 1 of 3

Start Network Log

Go to chrome://net-export, select "Include raw bytes", and click "Start Logging to Disk". Save the file.

NPPE: Step 2 of 3

Reload Policies

With logging active, open chrome://policy in a new tab and click "Reload policies". Then, return to the net-export tab and click "Stop Logging".

NPPE: Step 3 of 3

Analyze Log File

Upload the saved chrome-net-export-log.json file to find and display any administrator-configured networks.
